Our Roots

History of NMCS Kenya

The National Movement of Catholic Students Kenya (NMCS) is the Kenyan national branch of the International Movement of Catholic Students (IMCS) — Pax Romana, one of the oldest and most influential Catholic student organisations in the world, founded in 1921.

NMCS Kenya emerged from a growing recognition within the Church that students occupy a unique and prophetic role in society. Rooted in Catholic Social Teaching, the movement was established to provide a structured space for university and college students to live and grow in faith, while actively engaging with the social realities around them.

Guided by the See–Judge–Act methodology championed by Cardinal Cardijn, NMCS Kenya has formed generations of young Catholic leaders who go on to serve in professional, civic and church circles across Kenya and beyond.

"The student of today is the builder of tomorrow. NMCS exists to ensure that builder is anchored in the Gospel."
— IMCS–Pax Romana tradition

IMCS–Pax Romana holds consultative status with the United Nations and UNESCO, giving NMCS Kenya a global platform to speak on student rights, youth development and social justice at international levels.

Today, NMCS Kenya operates through chapters across universities and colleges nationwide, with Maseno University hosting one of the most vibrant and active chapters in the country.

The Maseno Chapter

The NMCS Maseno Chapter is home to passionate, faith-driven students at Maseno University. Led by an elected executive team, the chapter organises spiritual retreats, community outreach, academic forums and social justice campaigns throughout the academic year.
